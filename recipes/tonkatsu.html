Tonkatsu

Description

Tonkatsu is one of the many Western-inspired dishes that’s synonymous with Japanese comfort food. It’s composed of crispy, deep-fried pork cutlets, and it’s easier to make than you think. I’ll share my secrets for a light and airy breading and succulent, tender meat

Ingredients

  • Pork loin or pork tenderloin fillet – These cuts have an essential layer of fat that gives the meat an extra tender bite.
  • Salt and black pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • Large eggs
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Any neutral flavored oil, such as vegetable oil
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Black and white sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Make the dipping sauce:Grind the sesame seeds in a mortar and pestle. Divide the powder into small sauce bowls and top with tonkatsu sauce.
  2. Prepare the pork: Trim the excess fat and cut slits into the connective tissue. Pound the pork with a meat mallet, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Set up the breading station: Arrange three shallow trays with flour, beaten eggs whisked with oil, and panko. Prepare a deep pot with oil and heat on medium to 340ºF (170ºC).
  4. Bread the pork: Dredge the cutlets in flour, egg mixture, and panko.
  5. Fry: Cook one cutlet in the hot oil for 1 minute, then flip and cook for another minute. Transfer to a wire rack and fry the other cutlets.
  6. Fry a second time: Raise the oil temperature to 355°F (180°C), then fry one cutlet for 30 seconds on each side. Transfer to a wire rack and clean the oil with a fine-mesh skimmer. Repeat with the other cutlets.
  7. Cut and serve: Slice the cutlets into ¾-inch-thick pieces, then serve with the dipping sauce. I shred cabbage and serve it on the side.
